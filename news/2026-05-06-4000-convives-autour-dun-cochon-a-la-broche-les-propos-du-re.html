«4.000 convives autour d’un cochon à la broche» : les propos du recteur de la Grande Mosquée de Paris sur le Canon français dénoncés par la droite

Sources: europe1.fr / lejdd.fr / Valeurs actuelles

The rector of Paris's Grand Mosque has sparked political backlash after criticising France's "Canon français" – a government-backed cultural initiative.

According to reports, the rector made comments questioning the Canon's approach to French cultural identity, with right-wing politicians seizing on the remarks as evidence of ideological conflict over France's secular values and national heritage.

The Canon français is a state-endorsed list of essential French cultural works and figures designed to promote shared national identity. The initiative has drawn scrutiny from various quarters since its launch, with debates centring on whose cultural narratives it represents and how it reflects contemporary France.

The rector's comments have intensified existing tensions around how religious and secular institutions navigate France's commitment to laïcité – the constitutional principle separating religion from state affairs.

Right-wing opposition figures have denounced the remarks as incompatible with French values, framing the exchange as part of broader concerns about institutional integration and shared civic frameworks.

Muslim leaders and civil society organisations have not yet issued formal statements on the controversy at the time of reporting.

Source: Europe 1 / Le JDD
