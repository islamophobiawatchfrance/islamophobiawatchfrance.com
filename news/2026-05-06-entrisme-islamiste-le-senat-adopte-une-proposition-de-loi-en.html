« Entrisme islamiste » : le Sénat adopte une proposition de loi en citant un rapport et un sondage contestés sur les musulmans

Sources: Mediapart / Bladi.net

France's Senate has passed legislation targeting alleged Islamist infiltration of state institutions, drawing immediate criticism from Muslim organisations.

The upper house adopted the bill on Wednesday, introducing new vetting procedures for public sector employees and expanding monitoring of associations deemed threats to state neutrality and secular principles.

Critics, including Muslim groups cited by Bladi.net, have raised concerns about the legislation's scope and impact on Muslim communities. Mediapart reports the Senate relied on a contested report and survey about Muslims to justify the measure—sources that have faced scrutiny for methodological concerns.

Proponents argue the law protects France's laïcité, the constitutional separation of religion and state governance. The bill reflects ongoing legislative efforts since 2020 to address what the government characterises as political Islam's institutional presence.

Muslim representatives warn the legislation could disproportionately affect their communities through expanded administrative scrutiny and monitoring frameworks.
