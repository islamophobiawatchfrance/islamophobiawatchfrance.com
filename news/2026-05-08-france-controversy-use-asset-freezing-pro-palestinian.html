9 May 2026 · 1 min read

France is freezing the assets of pro-Palestinian activists, sparking controversy over the use of financial sanctions.

The measure targets individuals engaged in pro-Palestinian advocacy, raising questions about the scope and justification for asset freezing powers in the country.

Rights advocates have questioned whether the sanctions represent a proportionate response or constitute an overreach in restricting freedom of expression and assembly.

The controversy reflects broader tensions in France around protest activity and government use of financial enforcement tools against political activists.

Source: Anadolu Agency
