# Grand Mosque of Paris Announces Eid al-Adha 2026 Date as May 27

The Grand Mosque of Paris has officially announced that Eid al-Adha 2026 will be celebrated on May 27, according to announcements published by the institution and reported across French media outlets on Tuesday.

The Grand Mosque of Paris released an official communiqué announcing that Eid al-Adha 1447 Hijri (2026) will take place on May 27 in France, according to reports from Le Figaro, Le Parisien, Actu.fr, and Visas & Voyages Algérie published Tuesday morning and afternoon.

According to reporting from Visas & Voyages Algérie, the Grand Mosque of Paris indicated it would organize two prayer sessions for the celebration.

The official announcement from the Grand Mosque provides certainty for French Muslims regarding when the holiday will be observed nationally, eliminating ambiguity that can arise from regional variations in lunar sighting observations.

Reaction

The announcement enables French Muslim communities, employers, and local authorities to coordinate scheduling for the holiday period. Religious institutions and community organizations depend on such official dates to organize collective prayers and social gatherings.

French Muslim associations and mosque leadership have long advocated for clear, advance notice of Islamic holiday dates to facilitate workplace accommodations and educational planning. The Grand Mosque's early announcement for 2026 aligns with this need for advance planning.

Sources: Le Figaro, Le Parisien, Actu.fr, Visas & Voyages Algérie, Grande Mosquée de Paris

Q&A

When exactly is Eid al-Adha 2026 in France?
According to the Grand Mosque of Paris, Eid al-Adha 2026 will be celebrated on May 27.
Who determines the official date of Eid al-Adha in France?
The Grand Mosque of Paris serves as the primary institution announcing Eid al-Adha dates to the French Muslim community, as reported by Le Figaro, Le Parisien, and Actu.fr.
Why does the Eid date change each year?
Eid al-Adha is determined by the Islamic lunar calendar, which is approximately 11 days shorter than the Gregorian calendar, causing the holiday to shift earlier each year on the Western calendar.
How many prayer sessions will the Grand Mosque organize for Eid al-Adha 2026?
According to Visas & Voyages Algérie, the Grand Mosque of Paris will organize two prayer sessions for the celebration.
